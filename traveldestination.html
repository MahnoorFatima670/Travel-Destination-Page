Maldives Paradise

The Maldives are known as “the tropical paradise” for reasons such as its beauty. This tropical country has separated islands and islands are surrounded by its own lagoon. Each island has pure white sandy beaches. The sea covers 99% of the Maldives and it is where you can see beautiful fishes and corals.

Don't miss out on:

  • Crystal clear waters
  • Adventure activities
  • Local festivals

The Maldives is an independent island country in the north-central Indian Ocean. The islands that make up the country extend more than 510 miles (820 km) from north to south and 80 miles (130 km) from east to west. The northernmost atoll is about 370 miles (600 km) south-southwest of the Indian mainland.

Weather and Best Time to Visit

The Maldives enjoys a tropical climate with warm temperatures throughout the year. The best time to visit is from November to April, during the dry northeast monsoon season, when you can expect clear skies and calm seas.

However, if you're a fan of marine life and diving, consider visiting from May to October during the southwest monsoon. While there might be occasional rain showers, this is also the time when the waters are rich with marine biodiversity.

Keep in mind that weather can vary, so always check the latest forecasts before planning your trip.

Local Foods

Bis Keemia

Bis Keemiya is a delicious pastry filled with tuna, egg, spices, vegetables. This is so popular in the Maldives it is similar to rolls and samosa. The texture of the pastry is flakey and crispy from the outside and soft from the inside. The texture of the filling is so unique because the egg and tuna are soft and the vegetables are crispy. The pastry is a little sweet so it tastes really good with spiced filling. This is the best snack to have with coffee.

Mas Huni

A prevalent food in Maldives, Mas Huni - shredded smoked Tuna, served with grated coconuts, lemon and onions is a must-try. The dish is undoubtedly the most popular Maldivian breakfast, eaten with chapati bread called Roshi.
